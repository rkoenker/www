                      Graphics using Splus on the unix mainframe
          In the past it has been a headache for students to get hard copies of graphs created on the unix mainframe (ux6) using 
          S-Plus. Here is a help file on this in the stat329 web pages which may help:

          Goto  http://www.stat.uiuc.edu/~simpson/stat329  and choose:
          --> "Help with computing and graphics"
          --> "Printing and saving graphics"
          --> "Go here"

           It shows how to pull text and graphs out of X-windows, and how to paste text in. This page also has other information
           about running Splus from a lab site on campus. My current recommendation is to copy the graphs directly into Word or
           WordPerfect documents. These can be printed locally or saved to a diskette. It assumes you connect to the mainframe
           using a networked PC running Hummingbird  Exceed's X-windows software.
