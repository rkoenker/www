Caveat Emptor!

My Experience with Atlantic Retinning

This is a happy story, perhaps not the happiest ever told, but nevertheless happy enough to need expression. It all began in May when, after considerable deliberation it was decided in the highest councils of the family that it was time for our 17 inch copper saute pan to be retinned. Some googling determined that there was one obvious place to have this done, given that Bridge Kitchenware no longer seemed to be in the business, and that was Atlantic Retinning. In early May, 2004, with some trepidation this prized object was packaged up and send by UPS to Atlantic Retinning in Newark, New Jersey. "Our master craftsmen provide quick service with guaranteed satisfaction," claimed the web site, along with endorsements by such respected notable institutions as Martha Stewart, and the Russian Tea Room. No wonder there was trepidation.

In early June, my check, enclosed with the pan was cashed, and we expected to receive the pan at any moment, but when it did not arrive by mid-July, we began to become concerned. After that I called every other week. Each time I was told either: someone will call me right back, or that the pan is currently in process and will be returned in a "week or two". No pan, no phone call -- as of late August.

After suspending my weekly calls for a while, I decided to call September 15 for an update and was told that the pan "just needed to be polished" and would be shipped back on September 17.

September 28th: I called and spoke with Jaime, himself, who vaguely remembered shipping a pan to my address last week. Lo and behold! The pan arrived this afternoon! To celebrate we made the traditional Troisgrois entrecote dix sur dix and it was great. The pan is beautiful, and more importantly, restored to its full functionality. It was an excrutiating wait, but all my doubts have been erased. Those of you still waiting: Have faith better times are ahead!
