General Hypothesis Testing Procedure

Step 1:
Write down H0 and H1 (i.e. express the claim in terms of the population paramter(s)).
Step 2:
Identify the sample and test statistics (i.e. the evidence) to be used.

Step 3:
Determine the sampling distribution of the sample and/or test statistics under H0 (i.e. plot the distribution assuming H0 is true). Block out the acceptance and rejection regions in the domain of the sampling distribution.

Step 4:
If is given, determine the critical value(s) in terms of the sample and/or test statistics. If is not provided, find the p-value using the evidence as a critical value.

Step 5
Make decision and draw your conclusion in the context of the problem.
