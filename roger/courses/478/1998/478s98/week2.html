Week 2
Treatment Effect Estimation and IV Estimation for Quantile Regression



This week deals with the translation of the treatment literature into the domain of quantile regression. We will conclude the discussion of the Heckman paper (Deke), discuss a survey paper on IV results for returns to education by Card (Arias), briefly discuss Manski's Monotone treatment paper, and then proceed to the Abadie, Angrist and Imbens paper.

I would like to emphasize that there is no generally agreed upon strategy for extending IV estimation to quantile regression. The approach of Amemiya and Powell is probably most natural, but the direct estimating equation approach is also attractive in some ways. The special case considered by AAI deserves some close attention; it is not clear to me at this point how this is connected to earlier methods. Finally, I would like to mention that it would be very nice to have a successful generalization of LIML ideas into the quantile regression domain. There is some very interesting work along this line already by T. Sakata of Michigan, presented at the MEG meeting last fall. I might suggest the following references which will eventually appear in the course bibliography. Page references are given for the relevant sections in the three textbooks.

Koopmans, T.C. and W.C. Hood, (1953) Estimation of Simultaneous Linear Economic Relationships, in Hood and Koopmans (eds) Studies in Econometric method, Cowles Monograph 14 reprinted by Yale, 1970.

Chernoff, H. and H. Rubin (1953) Asymptotics Properties of LIML, in Hood and Koopmans (eds) Studies in Econometric method, Cowles Monograph 14 reprinted by Yale, 1970.

Goldberger, A. (1963) Econometric Theory, 338-346, Wiley.

Davidson, R. and J. MacKinnon (1993) Estimation and Inference in Econometrics, 644-651, Oxford.

Malinvaud, E. (1970) Statistical Methods in Econometrics, 692-706, North Holland.
