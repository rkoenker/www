Economics 478
Topics in Semiparametric Econometrics
Week 4
Inference for Quantile Regression



This week deals with the problem of statistical inference for quantile regression. I will present a new paper by Jose Machado and myself which deals with analogues of Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests based on the whole quantile regression process. There will also be some general discussion about other problems of inference associated with these methods. Some of this is covered in Chapter 3 of the distributed chapters.

The paper to be presented is available in pdf format here. The paper has been made into a working paper by BEBR and is available from Miki in room 484 of Commerce West. This way you don't have to worry about trying to print the pdf file.

I would like this to be a Chicago-style seminar, that is not simply a lecture, but a dialogue about the paper. This obviously presumes that you should read the paper carefully before class and come prepared to ask questions and make comments.

For those of you interested in following up the discussion about QR in Stata, I have located copies of three articles in the Stata Technical Bulletin which are relevant. I'll have these copied and you could pick up a copy in my office, if you are interested. The most recent one is from last summer and deals with the bootstrapping issue that we discussed last time. This might make an interesting paper topic for someone.

As another more or less random offering: I've put up a copy of Venables and Ripley's Programming Complements for their Splus book. I hope that this might be useful for a few people to supplement the material in my "Reproducability" paper. I hope that I can put up some additional material on the Goodness of Fit paper, this weekend.
