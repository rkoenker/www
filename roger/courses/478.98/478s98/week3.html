Week 3
Tutorial on Computation in Quantile Regression



This week deals with the problem of computational methods for quantile regression. This has been a long standing research problem, and the perceived computational inefficiency of quantile regression methods relative to least squares has been one of the major factors limiting the adoption of these methods.

I will provide an introduction to computational methods, discussing simplex based exterior point methods first, and then turning to recent developments in interior point methods. The basis for this tutorial will be the preliminary version of Chapter 6 of the monograph which I have been writing with Steve Portnoy. The current draft of this chapter is available in pdf format by clicking here. This has just expanded due to an unanticipated burst of productivity on my part, however closer examination will reveal that most of the progess consists in self-plagarism. Nevertheless, the current version which is now 45 pages is a first shot at a complete draft of the chapter. An abbreviated version of some of this material is available also in pdf format as an article for the ASA Statistical Computation and Graphics Newsletter. In addition, I would like to distribute a very concise Splus program which implements the basic algorithm of Barrodale and Roberts (1973) which has been the main computational engine for quantile regression until recently. For those who would like to see more detail on the interior point methods, you can find it on the research page devoted to our Statistical Science paper The Gaussian Hare and the Laplacian Tortoise. I would be especially interested in any comments you have on the the current chapter draft, or the associated Splus code obviously the latter is not very quick, and really can not expect to ever be, given the nature of language processing in Splus. But there is still the possibility of bugs, and I'm still looking for a better intuitive exposition of the weighted quantile algorithm. The appendix to the draft chapter attempts to explain this code.

I will also make some comments on extensions to nonlinear models, including the Powell censored regresssion model.
